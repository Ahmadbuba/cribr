The only troglodite house in Córdoba,the most exclusive in Andalusia Stands inside the stone quarry. We reach it via the enchanting 3000 m2 circular garden that surrounds it. Keeping the same temperature during the whole year from 18º to 20 ºC The space Casa Tierra The House is 14 km away from Córdoba This house stands inside the stone quarry. We reach it via the enchanting 3000 square metre circular garden that surrounds it. Crossing the white terrace, we move into a large well-lit room, where we are surprised by the raw stone walls of its structure. The harmony of its interior is due to the arrangement, colour and proportions of the furniture specially designed for the room, where every element of its construction displays the utmost respect for its original form. The bathroom and kitchen also open directly onto the garden, harmoniously combining the elements of stone, marble, steel, glass and soil. Relax here in an almost subterranean silence, while you contemplate the sky and trees through the window. The temperature stays at a constant 18 - 20 ºC all year round. Floor area: 90 m2 Terrace: 10 m2 Sleeps four Living/dining room Bedroom (latex mattresses 160 x 200 cm) Fully equipped kitchen Bathroom Heating Solar power A stay in the country close to Cordova, where you can discover our thousand-year-old World Heritage city, but with the tranquility of relaxing in your own pleasant house surrounded by nature, while you draw up your own schedule and enjoy your own culinary preferences. HISTORICAL NOTE According to archaeologists who have visited the site, this house once formed part of the quarry cave complex on the farm, and probably dates from the Roman era when Cordova was known as Colonia Patricia, but it could also date back to the quarries of the Arab period that supplied stone for the construction of the palatial city of Medinat al-Zahra and the Caliphal House of Ar-Rumaniya (both 10th century AD). During the 19th and 20th centuries, the area has been used for farming, conserving the buildings that housed stables and cattle barns, not to mention a bullring where bullfights were held with the farm’s own livestock, and attracting the most important bullfighters of the time The farm recently hosted the filming of the movie "Through the Eyes of Ouka Leele".
